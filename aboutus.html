About LeTS

The Learn Through Stories (LeTS) initiative reinforces the role of children as content creators rather than mere consumers, and enables them to learn about language, environment, culture, history and more, using storytelling and art as learning pedagogies.

The LeTS approach

  • gives a voice, identity, and a platform to the child's imagination
  • brings in confidence with the collaborative effort between the child and professional designers, animators, picture book artists and illustrators
  • is curated and tested by a team from IIT Bombay across schools in India

In collaboration with IIT Bombay, the Learn Through Stories (LeTS) Foundation aims to disseminate this alternate approach to learning - LeTS - by conducting content creation workshops and collecting contextual stories from schools in India, both rural and urban, to foster content co-creation, teach book design and production, and publish books that return to all students and teachers. Our products include a curated process of content creation, physical story books, and their digital parallels in the form of animation films and interactive applications. Our sole objective is to maximise social impact in the field of learning and education across India.

We, Alka Hingorani, Arti Kalro and Gayathri Thakoor, form the core team at Learn Through Stories (LeTS) Foundation.

Our Team

Alka Hingorani is Associate Professor in the School of Design (IDC), at IIT-Bombay. She is an architect and an art historian. She holds masters degrees in Design (Photography), City and Regional Planning, and a MA/PhD in History of Art, all from the University of California, Berkeley, where she also taught briefly before her return to India. Her research interests include art and identity, storytelling as academic practice, and pedagogy at the cusp of classroom teaching and technology. She loves poetry, pottery, the outdoors, and a scrap of solitude.
Gayathri Thakoor is a co-founder and the CEO of this social enterprise. A published author, manager, and entrepreneur, her years of starting out as a news journalist sharing people stories to finally signing off as Program Lead at the Tata Centre for Technology and Design team, at IIT Bombay, have seen rich experiences over 25 years. Taking the learnings out of these, she believes that collaboration is key. For better and more.
